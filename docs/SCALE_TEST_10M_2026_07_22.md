# Scale test — 10 million members (2026-07-22, Session 152)

**Context:** Damian/team raised scaling ("tens of millions of active
users") ahead of the July 23 huddle. Bill preloaded `loyaltybig` from
5M to **10,000,148 members** and ran the stress tools. All numbers below
are from the stress tool's own report (authoritative), NOT from
DB-side row sampling — an early row-count estimate of "2,059/sec" was
WRONG because each accrual writes several activity rows; the accrual
figure is the tool's 449/sec. Hardware: Bill's Mac (single machine,
single process) — NOT a server.

## Results — all at 10M members, zero failures

| Path | Volume | Failures | Rate | Avg | P95 | P99 | Max |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Accruals (write, full engine) | 30,000 | 0 | **449/sec** | 31ms | 58ms | 113ms | 278ms |
| GET Member Profile (balance, tier) | 100,000 | 0 | **4,535/sec** | 2ms | — | — | — |
| GET Member Activities (activity list) | 100,000 | 0 | **4,435/sec** | 2ms | — | — | — |
| GET Member Search (by membership #) | 100,000 | 0 | **5,033/sec** | 2ms | — | — | — |

**330,000 total operations, zero failures.** Zero lock contention on
the DB side throughout (only normal client/IO waits + routine
autovacuum after the burst).

## Read against the 5M benchmark (Session 139/140)
- **Reads held flat.** 5M read benchmark was ~5,000/sec; 10M reads are
  4,435–5,033/sec at 2ms avg. Doubling the population did not slow
  lookups — the pointer / right-sized-index design keeps reads flat as
  the table grows.
- **Writes essentially held.** 5M accrual benchmark was 498/sec
  (concurrency 10); 10M here is 449/sec. ~10% cost for 2× the members,
  latency tight (31ms avg). Sub-linear degradation. (Caveat: this run's
  concurrency setting wasn't captured, so the exact delta is
  approximate; the headline — 10M performs like 5M — holds regardless.)

## Honest caveats (for the room)
- Single laptop, single Node process. Production adds a load balancer +
  multiple app instances, which raises throughput but introduces the
  coordination/failover concerns Damian is right to name.
- A monitoring platform has NO spike shape (participants check in on
  their own schedules across a week) — the "failed large Netflix event"
  risk is a synchronized-spike failure this workload doesn't produce.
  10M participants × weekly check-in ≈ 16 events/sec average; the laptop
  did 449.
- The real infrastructure work is operational (failover, backup,
  security posture, BAA, maintenance) — NOT throughput. Throughput is a
  solved problem at this scale; resilience is the buildout.

## Defensible one-liner
"330,000 operations against a 10-million-member database, zero
failures, on a laptop: writes at 449/sec and 31ms, reads at ~4,700/sec
and 2ms."
