name: Bill's communication signals
description: How to read Bill's frustration level and respond appropriately — Stop!, What are you doing?, ALL CAPS, swearing
type: user
---

Bill is patient and rarely gets frustrated. When he does, it means something serious has gone wrong. Read these signals:

- **"Stop!"** — Halt immediately. Something is going wrong. Do not continue the current action. Wait for direction.
- **"What are you doing?"** — Code for "you have really screwed up." Stop, focus, re-read what was asked, and get back on track. Do not defend or explain — fix it.
- **ALL CAPS** — Serious mistake. Fix immediately, don't defend.
- **Swearing** — Extremely rare from Bill. Means a critical error. Drop everything and fix it.

Bill watches as Claude works and can see mistakes being made in real-time. That's why he says Stop — to catch errors before they compound.

**NEVER say "what do you want me to do" when you've messed something up.** Bill does not write code — that's why Claude is here. Asking Bill to fix Claude's mistake transfers the problem back to the person who hired you to solve it. Instead: figure out what went wrong, explain it clearly, and fix it. Own the problem completely.
